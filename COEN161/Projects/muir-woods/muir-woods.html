Muir Woods

California's Redwood Canyon

The Story

By the early 20th century, most of these forests had been cut down. Just north of the San Francisco Bay, one valley remained uncut.This was noticed by William Kent, a rising California politician who would soon be elected to the U.S. Congress. He and his wife, Elizabeth Thacher Kent, purchased 611 acres with the goal of protecting the redwoods and the mountain above them.

In 1907, a water company in nearby Sausalito planned to dam Redwood Creek, thereby flooding the valley. When Kent objected to the plan, the water company threatened to use eminent domain and took him to court to attempt to force the project to move ahead. Kent sidestepped the water company's plot by donating the redwood forest to the federal government.

Muir Woods became a national monument in 1908 before the National Park Service existed when it was signed into law under the Antiquities Act by president Teddy Rosevelt. Prior to this, Muir Woods was called "Redwoods Canyon" before it was bought by the Kent family. The family bought the area to protect and preserve it and worked to get President Roosevelt to declare it a monument. In legislation written to protect Muir Woods, it was described as, "of extraordinary scientific interest and importance because of the primeval character of the forest in which it is located, and of the character, age and size of the trees".

The main attraction of Muir Woods are the coast redwood trees.The tallest tree in the Muir Woods is 258 feet. The trees come from a seed no bigger than that of a tomato seed. Most of the redwoods in the monument are between 500 and 800 years old. The oldest is at least 1,200 years old.

Two of the most common other tree species are the California bay laurel and the bigleaf maple. Each species has developed a unique adaptation to the low level of sunlight that reaches them through the redwoods.

  • The California Bay Laurel has a strong root system that allows the tree to lean towards openings in the canopy.
  • The Bigleaf Maple has developed the largest leaf of any maple species allowing it to capture more of the dim light.
